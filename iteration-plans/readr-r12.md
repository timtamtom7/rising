# readr — Round 12: Social Features, Public Collections, Citation Export

## Goal
Add social and sharing features — public quote collections, citation export, and integration with academic tools — making Readr useful for both personal knowledge management and scholarly work.

---

## Scope

### Public Collections
- **Public collection profiles**: Share a collection of quotes publicly (opt-in)
- **Curated lists**: "The 50 Most Thought-Provoking Quotes from Books I Read This Year"
- **Collection following**: Follow public collections from authors, thinkers, curators
- **Featured collections**: Readr editorial team highlights best community collections

### Quote Social Feed
- **Anonymous quote sharing**: Share a quote to the public feed — no name attached, just the quote
- **Quote reactions**: Upvote quotes that resonate — "Most-loved quotes this week"
- **Quote threads**: Start a discussion about a specific quote
- **"What are you reading?"**: Weekly thread — share current book and favorite quote so far

### Citation Export
- **Citation formats**: Export quotes in APA, MLA, Chicago, Turabian, BibTeX, Harvard
- **PDF export**: Export a formatted PDF of quotes from a book or collection
- **Notion export**: Send quotes directly to a Notion page
- **Obsidian export**: Export as markdown to Obsidian vault
- **Readwise integration**: Sync quotes to Readwise for spaced repetition

### Academic Tools
- **Research project tracking**: Group quotes by research project or thesis
- **Bibliography generator**: Auto-generate bibliography from all quoted books
- **Quote verification**: AI verifies quote text against known book editions — flag potential misquotes
- **Page number tracking**: Track page numbers alongside quotes — critical for academic citation

---

## Out of Scope
- Platform expansion (Android, web) (R13)
- Subscription optimization (R14)
