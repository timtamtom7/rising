# blink — Round 17: Partnerships, B2B, White-Label, Enterprise

## Goal
Build B2B revenue streams by licensing Blink's technology to families, enterprises, and third-party platforms — opening a new business line beyond consumer subscriptions.

---

## Scope

### Family Pod (B2C Premium)
- Special "Family Pod" plan: a shared Blink vault for a family, managed by one parent
- Up to 6 family members can contribute
- Parent has admin: approve child clips before they go into shared album
- Child accounts (under 13): COPPA-compliant, parent-managed
- Family storage pooling: 500GB shared, $19.99/month

### Enterprise / Team Memory
- "Blink for Teams" — companies use Blink to capture company events, offsites, team moments
- Team vault: all company employees can contribute to team events
- Admin dashboard: manage team members, set recording policies
- No cloud storage on company side — all clips go to Blink's servers, team pays
- Pricing: $5/user/month, minimum 10 seats

### White-Label SDK
- License the Blink capture + playback SDK to other apps
- Use case: wedding planning apps (capture wedding moments), real estate (property tours), travel apps (trip capture)
- SDK: iOS + Android, MIT license for startup use, commercial license for larger companies
- White-label paywall handled by host app

### Integration Partnerships
- Apple Photos: option to sync Blink clips to Apple Photos library
- Google Photos: option to auto-backup Blink clips
- Wedding platforms (The Knot, Zola): embed Blink capture link in wedding registry
- Airbnb: hosts can capture property tour moments for guests
