# kale — Round 18: Platform Ecosystem, Wearables, Spatial Computing, Vision

## Goal
Extend Kale into a full platform ecosystem — integrating with wearables, launching for visionOS, and building the long-term technology vision for Kale as a comprehensive health intelligence platform.

---

## Scope

### Wearable Integration
- **Apple Watch Ultra**: Kale as the supplement + health companion for endurance athletes
- **Oura Ring**: Pull HRV, sleep, temperature data — correlate with supplement intake
- **Whoop**: Pull recovery score, strain — correlate with supplements
- **Continuous glucose monitoring (CGM)**: Dexcom integration — does Vitamin D affect blood glucose? Real data.
- **Biometric correlation dashboard**: See how supplements affect HRV, sleep, recovery in real-time

### Kale for Apple Vision Pro
- **Spatial health dashboard**: View your health data in 3D spatial space
- **Supplement information**: Point to a supplement bottle in your kitchen — see its info overlay
- **AR supplement guide**: When traveling, point camera at foreign supplement — AI identifies and explains
- **Immersive wellness**: Guided supplement routines in a calming spatial environment

### Platform Ecosystem
- **Kale API**: REST API for third-party developers to access supplement data (with user consent)
- **Integration marketplace**: Certified integrations with health apps (MyFitnessPal, Cronometer, Oura)
- **Supplement data API**: Sell aggregate, anonymized supplement data to health researchers

### Long-Term Vision (2027-2030)
- **Predictive health**: Kale predicts health risks before they manifest — "Your Vitamin D has been low for 3 months, risk of X is elevated"
- **Personalized nutrition**: Kale + gut microbiome test + genetics → truly personalized supplement recommendations
- **Preventive health standard**: Kale as part of routine preventive health check — like a blood test you take daily
- **Kale for clinical trials**: Pharma companies use Kale to track supplement adherence in clinical trials
- **Global supplement intelligence**: Kale's aggregate data shows supplement trends worldwide — "70% of users in Nordic countries are Vitamin D deficient in January"
